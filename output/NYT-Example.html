When systems overheat, physics demands a return to balance. Democracies are no different.

In radar work, one degree of misalignment can fill the screen with lies. That’s what happened to America. Our civic array tilted, our returns blurred. Politics became an echo chamber of noise.

Donald Trump didn’t invent the chaos, but he amplified it. The nation’s Intention Field—the invisible geometry that keeps a society coherent—shifted out of phase.

Enter Zohran Mamdani, son of immigrants, mayoral frontrunner, and—mathematically speaking—a coherence attractor. Where Trump radiated volatility, Mamdani projects equilibrium.

IV. The AI Torque Problem

While one system heals, another threatens to shear the gearbox. Artificial intelligence and robotics add unprecedented torque to the economy, but torque without redistribution is just mechanical stress.

Equation 3 — Productive Surplus Equilibrium
d(UCIP)/dt = φ·(shared benefit) − D·(displacement)
φ = AI surplus; D = job displacement. Sharing surplus through public options, data dividends, and worker voice stabilizes the field.

V. Frequency and the Human Brain

Trump tuned the national psyche to ~20 Hz, the anxious beta band. Mamdani’s coherence hums closer to ~10 Hz, the calm alpha wave. That’s not metaphor—it’s resonance. Societies, like brains, seek rhythm.

VI. The Field Repairs Itself

Every chaotic phase generates its inverse. The same laws that govern feedback loops in circuitry apply to public life: energy disperses, equilibrium returns. New York’s election is not just municipal politics. It’s proof that local coherence can retune a continental field.

If we extend that same physics to AI governance and economic design, the math stops describing balance—and starts ensuring it.

The system isn’t broken. It’s merely out of tune. And the nation, at last, is learning to hear itself again.
